Qualifications

   Throughout my education at Texas A&M, I have been given many opportunities—inside and outside of class—to further my experience with various technologies. My first experience with programming, and computer science in general, was with Python; I took an introductory class freshman year and continue to use it outside of class for mathematical proofs, side projects, and to further my understanding of algorithms. I have taken 3 classes that have used C/C++ as their primary language, and consider myself to be fairly comfortable in these lower-level languages. Other languages/technologies that classes have covered include; Java, Haskell, SQL, PHP, ARMv8 Assembly, and BASH Script.


   Outside of school, an internship during the summer of 2022 provided me with an amazing opportunity to gain more experience. I spent the summer learning the basics of web development and became comfortable with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and additionally learned a great deal about Shopify's Liquid Template Language. I continue to practice with these technologies to become more familiar with full-stack development, in fact, this website was built using only HTML, CSS, and JavaScript! While I have learned a lot since coming to Texas A&M, I am not done learning; I like to adopt a mindset of constant learning otherwise I would get bored.


   Luckily for me, Computer Science is such a broad field with so many applications, it would be fairly difficult to get bored. While I can see the many applications of my major and the various routes I could go with it, I admit that I still struggle to know exactly which field I want to pursue after graduation. This is the primary reason for my interest in software consulting—as a consultant, you work on projects for such a wide variety of clientele that you can learn more about what areas of computer science you enjoy, and which you don't. I also enjoy working with others, and by doing consulting I would put myself in an environment where working with others is an everyday event: I would get opportunities to discuss projects with both clients and coworkers. This prospect of a wide variety of projects and the emphasis on social interaction makes consulting my main area of interest in the field of computer science, and something I believe I would like to do upon graduation from Texas A&M University.
