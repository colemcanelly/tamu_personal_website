Processor Design

During the fall of my Junior year at Texas A&M, I took a class for Computer Engineers called "Computer Architechture"; in which, the labs had us focusing on designing a processor. This semester-long project was completed during lab time and culminated in the design and simulation of a processor capable of running a subset of ARMv8 Assembly. (Repository)


During this project I learned how to write ARM assembly and integrate it with compiled C code, and furthered my knowledge of VerilogHDL and concepts surrounding circuit design. The first few weeks were spent learning assembly, and the remainder of the semester was spent describing various components of our processor in Verilog and using unit tests we had written to ensure our components integrity. Finally, we were able to integrate and simulate all of the components where we could insert assembled code into the instruction memory and run various programs.
